## Chapter in Brief

**Central thesis:** Prose humor applies the same joke mechanics without a live performer, using textual brevity, arrangement, punctuation, sentence length, and implied voice to guide a solitary reader who controls pacing and may become a collaborator online.

Kalan defines prose broadly as any joke delivered in text to be looked at or felt, from books and tweets to signs and packaging [Page 228]–[Page 232]. Short online forms enable premises that would collapse under expansion, while invented textual personas can become more extreme because no live body needs to sustain them [Page 232]–[Page 235]. Layout and sentence structure partially recover timing: line breaks, lists, periods, and long sentences shape attention, acceleration, pause, and disruption [Page 235]–[Page 240].

Tone is fragile without performance, so reading aloud and clarifying the implied relationship with the reader become important [Page 240]–[Page 241]. Online prose also blurs teller and audience as comments, remixes, and reusable joke formats turn personal expression into communal structure—potentially extending connection while complicating authorship and compensation [Page 243]–[Page 244].

**Essential points**

- Prose is text-delivered humor, not only literary print [Page 231]–[Page 232].
- Concise online forms legitimize quick, grammar-bending ideas that do not support essays, songs, or stand-up setups [Page 232].
- Visual spacing and sentence length are structural tools for rhythm [Page 235]–[Page 240].
- With no performer to repair meaning, tone must be encoded and tested in the text [Page 240]–[Page 241].
- Online audiences can transform a finished joke into an iterative template [Page 243]–[Page 244].

## Detailed Walkthrough

### Text without performance

A prose joke must provoke laughter without vocal emphasis, gesture, or crowd contagion [Page 228]–[Page 231]. Kalan’s definition includes packaging and social media because medium is less important than text being the delivered object [Page 231]. The damaged Old Spice transcription exemplifies packaging humor but should not be reconstructed from the fragment.

Short posts preserve ideas whose funny charge depends on appearing as a sudden thought. Expanding watercolor-hen, Athena/spider, or “four eels” jokes would create diminishing returns or require an artificial spoken introduction [Page 232]. Jack Handey and @dril use different grammar but share an “oblivious sociopath” voice, three-part movement, precise detail, and clear brevity [Page 234]–[Page 235].

### Space and pace

Text’s special variable is physical arrangement. A vertical budget list isolates each plausible cost, reveals an absurd candle expense, then returns to a low utility cost before switching into prose; the visual change reinforces the structural disruption [Page 235]–[Page 236]. Zadie Smith’s stacked comparisons similarly make a character’s insignificance visible [Page 236]–[Page 237].

Readers control speed, but writers influence it. Long unbroken sentences build momentum toward a strong stop; repeated short sentences create accumulating staccato impacts. Empty lines and list formatting force resets, functioning like pauses [Page 237]–[Page 240].

### Tone and communal afterlife

Prose irony is easily mistaken for sincerity because the writer cannot read the room. Formal distance can make a speaker huffy and closed, while rhetorical questions, self-mockery, and “we” create conversational closeness. Kalan’s practical test is to read the work aloud, revise emotional or verbal ambiguity, and repeat [Page 240]–[Page 241].

Online platforms allow the audience to reply with jokes and turn a post into a refillable form. The Hannibal template lets users replace topical setup or historical reset, depersonalizing authorship while creating teller–audience and audience–audience connections [Page 243]–[Page 244]. Kalan presents this as possibility and frustration, not an unqualified economic good.

## Practical Applications

- Match length to premise; do not expand a quick textual incongruity solely to justify a larger form [Page 232].
- Use line breaks, sentence length, and punctuation to control where the eye pauses or accelerates [Page 235]–[Page 240].
- Read prose jokes aloud to detect tonal and rhythmic effects that silent drafting hides [Page 240]–[Page 241].

## Assumptions, Nuances, and Limitations

Reader pace remains only partially controllable. Online remixing can create connection but may also feel like heckling, loss of ownership, or uncompensated appropriation [Page 243]–[Page 244].

## Takeaways

Prose is not performance-minus; its visible text supplies its own timing and persona. The most distinctive opportunity is to design structures that remain funny both as authored statements and as forms readers can inhabit.
