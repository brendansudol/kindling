# 31. Pushing Yourself

## Header

**Book:** *Finding Your Comic Genius*  
**Author:** Adam Bloom  
**Chapter:** 31 — Pushing Yourself  
**Source range:** Location 3971 of 4548–Location 4019 of 4548  
**Source status:** complete.

## Chapter in Brief

**Central thesis:** Use comfortable gigs as controlled laboratories for growth, while reserving reliable material for conditions where the audience has already absorbed substantial risk.

Bloom proposes rotating challenges: bespoke or improvised openings, temporarily dropping the strongest bit, trying new lines, changing profanity placement, and testing a new closer [Locations 3986–3992]. He recommends this roughly fortnightly after six months when gigs are not consistently difficult.

## Detailed Walkthrough

Comfort can produce excellent shows but also long-term stagnation. Bloom therefore separates the audience-facing goal of delivering value from the career-facing need to experiment, choosing favorable rooms for the latter [Locations 3986–3992]. He warns against combining risky new material with a late, already difficult show: artistic freedom does not erase obligations to ticket-buyers [Location 3992].

## Takeaways

Risk should be deliberate, recurring, and context-sensitive. Protect the audience in hard conditions; remove one safety net when conditions are strong enough to teach you something.
