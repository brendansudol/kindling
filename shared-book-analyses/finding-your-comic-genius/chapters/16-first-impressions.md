## Chapter in Brief

**Central thesis:** The entrance, handling of stage objects, movement, appearance, and opening line can communicate persona and style before an audience has time to form a weaker default impression.

Bloom analyzes small pre-verbal behaviors and extremely economical openings. The objective is not theatrical busyness but purposeful alignment: every choice should reveal the same person and make the opening feel present to this room [Locations 2203–2244].

## Detailed Walkthrough

Denis Leary dragging a microphone stand, Dylan Moran shaking a microphone, and Gavin Webster holding it like an electrician all make attitude visible before speech [Locations 2203–2212]. A grumpy newcomer’s early watch glance similarly works because it confirms that she does not want to be there; an unmotivated watch check instead makes time reappear for an audience that had forgotten it [Location 2212].

Bloom’s audit covers mic handling, MC greeting, audience acknowledgment, requested introduction, walk-on music, and movement. Jack Dee’s sarcastic headache response immediately establishes grumpiness, while Emo Philips’s “great to be in…doors” establishes misfit status through a final-syllable misdirection. Both respond to the present situation while compressing persona and style into one line [Locations 2223–2244]. Kitty Flanagan’s opening impersonation of prejudice against a female comedian disarms a suspected objection by voicing it first [Location 2244].

Conversational comedians should avoid a “needle hitting the record.” Bloom recommends a bespoke observation, a response to the introduction, or briefly clearing the mind to notice the room, with a prepared opening as safety net. MC work develops the ability to move between spontaneous events and prepared speech without saying “back to my set,” which would expose the seam [Location 2269].

The closing checklist asks whether outfit, approach, opening line, and style all communicate within 30 seconds. Bloom treats four-way alignment as an aspirational bar; two or three signals still help [Location 2295].

## Practical Applications

Record or rehearse the first 30 seconds from name announcement onward. Inventory the four signals, remove generic behavior, and write an opener restricted to both the immediate room and the intended persona/style [Locations 2223; 2269; 2295].

## Takeaways

An opening is not preliminary administration. It is the first concentrated demonstration that movement, language, status, and persona belong to the same act.
