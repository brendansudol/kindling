## Chapter in Brief

**Central thesis:** Repeating or echoing a final key word usually weakens surprise unless the repetition is deliberately structured as bookending or as a musical sound reversal.

Bloom formalizes four conditions for bookending and distinguishes it from “flipping,” then extends the sound audit to partial repetition and adjacent matching syllables [Locations 2830–2915]. The larger point is musical: set-ups as well as punchlines should be pleasurable language, without sounding artificially written [Location 2942].

## Detailed Walkthrough

An early draft repeated “manual” shortly before ending on the same word; changing the second term to “automatic” restored surprise and produced a harder sound [Locations 2819–2830]. Bookending is the major exception: identical key words appear at the start and finish of a sufficiently long second movement, with the first immediately before a pause. Distance lets the second occurrence behave like a mini-callback [Locations 2830–2853].

Bloom’s intentionally weak dog joke still gains punch from this architecture. Moving the first “dog” away from the pause or reducing the distance destroys the effect and also removes anticipation [Locations 2853–2878]. If a bookend sounds wrong, check the four conditions or substitute a synonym for one occurrence, preferably preserving the strongest final word [Locations 2878–2890].

“Flipping” repeats a sound across reversed phrases—“objectifies women / humanises objects.” It is gentler and more poetic than bookending, useful in lulls and set-ups when the phrases remain close enough to hear as a pair [Locations 2904–2915]. Partial repetition near a final word (“someone/anyone”) can still weaken an ending. Yet adjacent identical syllables may create flow (“usually totally”) where merely similar ones (“usually extremely”) clash [Locations 2915–2942].

## Practical Applications

Read every ending aloud; search backward for the final word or its final syllable; remove accidental echoes; preserve repetition only when the bookending conditions or a clear musical flip are audible. Keep ornament subtle enough for the persona [Locations 2878–2942].

## Takeaways

The last word should normally arrive as sound as well as information. Repetition becomes strength only when rhythm makes the return itself the event.
