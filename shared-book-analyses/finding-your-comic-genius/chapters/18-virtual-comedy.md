# 18. Virtual Comedy

## Header

**Book:** *Finding Your Comic Genius*

**Source range:** Location 2363 of 4548–Location 2430 of 4548  
**Source status:** complete.

## Chapter in Brief

**Central thesis:** Recognition can imitate the response to invention; comedians should avoid getting laughs by merely repackaging an existing joke, misunderstanding a logical feature, or reminding audiences of something already funny.

Bloom calls these shortcuts “virtual comedy.” His three categories are joking about something already intended as a joke, alleging a flaw without understanding its function, and provoking memory of prior comedy without adding an original spin [Locations 2363–2400].

## Detailed Walkthrough

A child’s “frog’s favorite game is leapfrog” line anchors the distinction: the answer repeats the game’s originating joke rather than making a new connection [Location 2363]. Category A mirrors an already playful shower-gel label and claims ownership of the reflected humor. Category B mocks a guide-dog exception sign while ignoring that it instructs sighted dog owners. Both project superiority over something the speaker has failed to understand [Locations 2377–2400].

Category C says a known comic program is crazy and lets the audience laugh at its memory. Bloom carefully separates this from observational comedy, which makes a previously unexamined behavior newly visible. Existing funny material can legitimately become a springboard if the comedian contributes analysis, transformation, or an outstanding act-out [Locations 2400–2428].

Bloom concedes that not all comedy must be verbally inventive: performance, eccentricity, and personality can themselves create value. His objection is to claiming wit where no new contribution exists and to accepting a knee-jerk response that leaves nothing memorable [Location 2428].

## Practical Applications

For material about an already funny object, phrase, or program, identify exactly what new thought, transformation, performance, or perspective the act contributes. If the answer is only “it reminds people,” use it as a set-up for creation rather than a finished joke [Locations 2400–2428].

## Takeaways

Do not confuse a reliably triggered laugh with authored comedy. The relevant quality test is what the performer created beyond the audience’s preexisting association.
