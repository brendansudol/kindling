## Chapter in Brief

**Central thesis:** Treat audience interjections according to intent rather than reflexively as attacks, respond within persona, and bridge the altered room state before returning to prepared material.

Bloom’s strongest practical tool is a prepared short story about chaos: after addressing an interruption, that story absorbs the room’s remaining energy and leads back to the original agenda [Locations 3509–3574].

## Detailed Walkthrough

Julian Barratt turns an aggressive “Goodnight!” into an apparent question with “Not really, no,” instantly converting a collapsing gig into high energy without leaving character [Locations 3455–3470]. Bloom argues that many so-called heckles are attempts to contribute. His percentages are explicitly optimistic estimates; their purpose is to condition a less defensive first reading [Locations 3470–3496].

Eddie Murphy laughing with a funny audience member models security, while an MC’s “I’ll do the jokes” after a student supplied a better punchline punishes positive participation and makes later warmth seem false [Locations 3496–3509]. Persona can support brutality, but the full audience judges whether it was deserved.

A magician’s card trick fails after security removes a disruptive guest because the performer ignores the scuffle and demands attention immediately. Bloom proposes commentary, shared acknowledgment, or—most generally—a brief chaos story. The sequence is: chaos → address chaos → chaos-themed material → original agenda [Locations 3523–3574]. Preparing one or two 45-second stories supplies a “skeleton key” for situations where improv confidence is low.

## Practical Applications

Classify interjections as hostile, destabilizing, offended, or contributory; respond consistently with persona; prepare a short chaos story; and do not begin unrelated material until the audience has processed the interruption [Locations 3470–3574].

## Takeaways

The interruption itself rarely determines the gig. The performer’s interpretation, proportionality, and transition determine whether chaos becomes connection or a lingering clunk.
