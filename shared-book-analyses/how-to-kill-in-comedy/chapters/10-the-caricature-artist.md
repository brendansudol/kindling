# Chapter in Brief

**Central thesis:** The safest first formula for character-driven material is to enlarge a truthful flaw either by degree or by moving it into an absurd context, then express the result through a boast or defense that supplies the Blind Spot.

The chapter repackages Part One's eyebrow method as a repeatable joke-writing procedure. A mild admission such as being too large for a doorway is heightened to appearing on Google Earth, then defended as needing to lose only “a touch” of weight [Pages 79–81]. Two generation routes—extreme behavior and insane location—lead into separate worksheets, followed by a third worksheet that adds the Blind Spot [Pages 81–88].

Essential points:

- Begin with a flaw already identified in Chapter 4; this keeps the output anchored in persona [Pages 79–81].
- Heighten the scale or transplant the behavior to the “dumbest” possible situation [Pages 81, 86].
- Bragging usually expresses Blind Spot more strongly than defending, though both are supported [Pages 81, 86].

# Detailed Walkthrough

## Exaggerate beyond recognition, retain the root

North's weight example moves from plausible inconvenience to impossible planetary scale; the sexually insecure character uses dollhouse furniture to boast about apparent size [Pages 79–81]. These examples preserve a recognizable insecurity while making the action fictional. The output must surprise, not simply describe the original flaw.

## Two enlargement methods

The first route grossly enlarges conduct: a possessive person avoids the bathroom to keep everything or preserves fingernail clippings [Pages 81, 84]. The second changes context: talkativeness moves into a library, flirtation into a colonoscopy, competitiveness into a children's league [Page 86]. The worksheet question—where would this trait be ridiculous, outrageous, or illegal?—forces incongruity rather than mere intensity.

## Blind Spot as final pass

The last worksheet asks the writer to narrate odd behavior as reasonable, admirable, or necessary. Holding waste becomes being good at preserving important things; maintaining contact with a robber becomes social skill; keeping clippings becomes prudent future planning [Page 86]. The character does not explain the joke but misinterprets the evidence.

## Polish after structure

North closes by encouraging repeated setup-and-punchline wording experiments [Page 88]. The formula is recommended for beginners and as a method to revisit because every result begins with a truth about the performer rather than a detached topic.

# Frameworks, Processes, and Mental Models

1. List specific flaws.
2. For each, either exaggerate conduct far beyond reality or move it to an absurd place/situation.
3. Add Blind Spot by bragging or defending.
4. Refine wording and timing [Pages 84–88].

# Takeaways

Caricature Artist is the bridge between character analysis and joke production. It does not ask “What topic should I write about?” but “What would my established flaw do at impossible scale or in the worst possible setting?”
